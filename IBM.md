# Tomorrow's topic :-

## What is _AI_?

Artificial Intelligence is defined in the video as a branch of Computer Science focused on creating intelligent machines that can work and react like humans. The emphasis is on human-like behavior—systems performing tasks (like bringing coffee, recognizing patterns, or making decisions) in a way comparable to human actions.

## Why should you learn about _AI_?

The speaker frames AI as one of the most exciting technological advancements currently happening. It represents a major shift in how systems are built and used, with real-world implementations already present (automation, robotics, smart systems). Studying it places you at the center of an ongoing technological evolution.

## Why is _AI_ dominating every domain?

Because AI is already embedded in practical, everyday applications—ranging from automated devices (like coffee machines and delivery systems) to predictive models and smart home systems. Its ability to replicate intelligent behavior and improve efficiency across tasks is why it is rapidly expanding into multiple domains.

## What are the types of _AI_?

The video outlines four categories:

- **Reactive Machines**: Basic systems with no memory, designed for specific tasks.
- **Limited Memory**: Systems that use past data and experiences (e.g., self-driving cars).
- **Theory of Mind**: Experimental systems aiming to understand human emotions and interactions.
- **Self-aware AI**: Future systems expected to be conscious and highly intelligent.

## Use of _AI_ in your domain?

Applications shown include prediction systems (like determining diabetes risk using medical data), image classification, price prediction (e.g., airline tickets), and smart environments (lights, TVs, sensors reacting to human presence or voice). These demonstrate AI’s role in automation, decision-making, and pattern recognition.
